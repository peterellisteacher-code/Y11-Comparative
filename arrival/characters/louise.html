They carry different assumptions about intent, so she refuses to collapse them. Her use of ‘culture’ to describe language suggests that, for Louise, words are not just symbols; they carry the messiness of the people who use them.

Understand / Child — Louise is watching a vision of her future daughter and her word choice betrays her confusion. ‘Understand’ is the linguist’s instinctive verb; she reaches for comprehension before emotion.
